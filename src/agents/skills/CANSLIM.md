# CANSLIM Analysis Skill

CANSLIM is a strategy for selecting growth stocks developed by William J. O'Neil. It combines both fundamental and technical analysis.

## The CANSLIM Acronym
- **C: Current Quarterly Earnings**: Look for stocks with recent quarterly earnings per share (EPS) up at least 25%.
- **A: Annual Earnings Increases**: Look for stocks with annual earnings growth of at least 25% over the last 3-5 years.
- **N: New Products, Management, or Highs**: Look for companies with new products, services, leadership, or reaching new price highs.
- **S: Supply and Demand**: Look for stocks with strong demand (high volume) and a limited supply of shares.
- **L: Leader or Laggard**: Focus on market leaders with a high Relative Strength Rating (80 or above).
- **I: Institutional Sponsorship**: Look for stocks with increasing ownership by institutional investors (mutual funds, etc.).
- **M: Market Direction**: Only buy stocks during a confirmed market uptrend.

## Execution Rules
1. Only buy stocks meeting most or all CANSLIM criteria.
2. Cut losses quickly if a stock drops 7-8% below your purchase price.
3. Add to positions as the stock rises and shows strength.
