# Volume Spread Analysis (VSA) Skill

Volume Spread Analysis (VSA) is a method of technical analysis that seeks to understand the relationship between price action and volume to determine the activity of "smart money" or professional traders.

## Core Principles
1. **Supply and Demand**: The market moves based on the balance of supply and demand created by professional players.
2. **Cause and Effect**: A period of accumulation or distribution (cause) leads to a subsequent price trend (effect).
3. **Effort vs. Result**: Volume represents effort. If there is high volume (effort) but little price movement (result), it indicates a potential trend reversal.

## Key Concepts
- **Accumulation**: Professional buying at lower prices.
- **Distribution**: Professional selling at higher prices.
- **Stopping Volume**: High volume on a down bar that stops the downward movement, indicating professional buying.
- **No Demand**: Low volume on an up bar, indicating professional traders are not interested in higher prices.
- **No Supply**: Low volume on a down bar, indicating professional traders are not interested in lower prices.
- **Upthrust**: A bar with a wide spread, high volume, and a close near the low, often indicating a peak.
